A DYING UNDERWATER WORLD

Coral reefs are some of the most valuable ecosystems on Earth and they are dying rapidly. 70% of the world's coral reefs have already been destroyed and these ecosystems are expected to disappear altogether over the next four decades. Some of the greatest threats to coral reefs include overfishing, pollution, ocean acidification, and climate change.

COLLAPSE OF THE CARIBBEAN

The Caribbean used to be an ideal breeding ground for corals where they could thrive and grow quickly. Unfortunately, the Caribbean reefs are mostly dead today as coral cover in the area has plummeted to less than 10%. The Caribbean reefs are the first system to experience such a collapse and many fear that other reefs may follow.
